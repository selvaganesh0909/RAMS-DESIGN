Selection & Installation

The overall effect was far superior to the first brick, and these softer looking bricks were also much more in keeping with the weathered originals, so an exact recreation was discounted. As a palette of materials in themselves I think they’re also much more attractive.
It’s not just the softer look of the brick, but the black flecks in the odd brick also pull it together with the paving and the flints. Once chosen we could start building the walls. This was one of the most exciting parts of the project as it was the first time, after weeks of work, that we’d see final finishes going in. A similar selection process was undertaken with the mortar too, with four different mixes trialled with the materials in order to use a colour that would make the most of the bricks and the flints and, again, be sympathetic to the original property.
